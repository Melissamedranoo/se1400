Trinkets like Labubus, blind box toys, keychains, and small collectible figurines often become performative because they are less about function and more about display and identity. People do not just own them, they arrange them on shelves, photograph them, and include them in posts that signal a certain aesthetic or personality. The appeal is partly in the cuteness or rarity, but also in what the collection says about the person who has it. A carefully curated display can suggest taste, trend awareness, or being part of a specific online culture where collecting is a shared language. Even choosing which ones to buy or unbox can feel like a statement, especially when certain characters or styles are tied to trends. Over time, the trinkets stop being just objects and start acting like props in a larger image, where the way they are shown matters just as much as the enjoyment of owning them.
