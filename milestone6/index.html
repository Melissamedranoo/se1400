The Goat of Performance

They go everywhere with a matcha in hand and a tote bag on their shoulder, like it is just part of who they are. They play Clairo in the background or casually bring up their Labubu figure like it is nothing, but it all feels a little too put together to be random. They always seem to know the right thing to wear, the right thing to say, the right way to exist in a moment so it looks natural from the outside. It is that kind of effortless vibe that does not actually happen by accident. You can tell it has been thought through, even if they would never admit it. Even the smallest details feel intentional, like the way they hold their drink or what they choose to post or mention in conversation. None of it is fake exactly, but it never feels fully unfiltered either. It feels like everything is chosen with an audience in mind, like they are always a little bit “on,” even when they are trying to seem relaxed.
