Aesthetic Over Taste

Performative people tend to gravitate toward foods that look as intentional as they feel, things like matcha lattes, açai bowls, sourdough toast, or anything that photographs well in soft lighting. It is not that these foods are unusual or bad, but they have become tied to a certain aesthetic that signals taste, health, or lifestyle awareness. A matcha is not just a drink, it suggests calm discipline and curated simplicity. An açai bowl is not just breakfast, it becomes a visual statement of wellness and effort. Even something as ordinary as avocado toast can feel performative when it is chosen and shared less for enjoyment and more for how it fits into a recognizable image. The performance comes from the intention behind the choice, where food becomes part of identity building rather than just eating. Posting it, styling it, or even casually mentioning it turns a basic meal into a signal, like saying you are part of a certain culture or aesthetic without having to say it directly.
